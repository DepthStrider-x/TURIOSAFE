Assam

Land of Tea Gardens, Rhinos, and the Mighty Brahmaputra

Overview

Assam, located in Northeast India, is known for its rolling tea estates, the Brahmaputra River, Kaziranga National Park, and vibrant cultural festivals like Bihu. It is a gateway to the Northeast and offers both natural beauty and spiritual heritage.

Best Places to Visit

Kaziranga National Park

UNESCO site, home to the one-horned rhinoceros.

Majuli Island

World’s largest river island, hub of Assamese culture.

Guwahati

City on the Brahmaputra with Kamakhya Temple.

Sivasagar

Historic Ahom kingdom sites and monuments.

Manas National Park

Biodiversity hotspot and UNESCO World Heritage site.

Pros & Cons of Visiting

Pros

  • Rich wildlife (rhinos, elephants, birdlife)
  • Unique culture, festivals, and dance forms
  • Scenic tea gardens and river landscapes
  • Peaceful offbeat destinations

Cons

  • Heavy monsoon rains cause flooding
  • Limited infrastructure in rural areas
  • Remote locations may need extra travel time
  • Seasonal restrictions for national parks (closed in monsoon)
